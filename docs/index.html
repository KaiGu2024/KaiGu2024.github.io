Research

Research Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of the economics of information and digital technologies. I study how artificial intelligence and digital platforms are reshaping information ecosystems, and trace the downstream consequences for markets and society.

My current work spans two related threads:

The AI divide. How do consumers from different demographic and socioeconomic backgrounds differentially adopt, use, and benefit from AI-enabled services, and what does this imply for inequality in AI-mediated markets?

AI and the information environment. How does generative AI reshape information access, knowledge production, and cultural production — and how do these shifts reallocate attention, referral traffic, and revenue across platforms, publishers, and content creators?

I’m also broadly interested in how political ideology and social norms govern the production and consumption of cultural goods in an increasingly polarized world.

To study these questions, I combine economic theory with computational methods, including causal inference, machine learning, and natural language processing, to extract insights from large-scale structured and unstructured data.
