Research

Research Interests

My research interests sit at the intersection of economics, technology, and culture, with two main streams:

Digital technologies & society: This stream studies how digital platforms and AI reshape information distribution and the downstream consequences for markets, politics, and society.

Culture, ideology & norms: This stream studies how political ideology and social norms govern cultural production and consumption, and how cultural markets in turn shape those social forces.

To answer these questions, I combine economic theory with computational methods, including causal inference, machine learning, and natural language processing, to extract insights from large-scale structured and unstructured data.

Causal Inference

Panel Data Causal Tools: fect, interflex, fdid

Yiqing Xu · Stanford / Princeton

fect — counterfactual estimators for panel data with interactive fixed effects, generalizing DiD and synthetic control.
interflex — diagnostic tools for multiplicative interaction models; detects linearity violations and plots marginal effects.
fdid — Factor-augmented DiD that removes latent common shocks without requiring parallel trends.

Author site

Double / Debiased Machine Learning (DML)

Uses Neyman-orthogonal moment conditions and cross-fitting to estimate low-dimensional treatment effects while flexibly controlling for high-dimensional nuisance functions via ML.
